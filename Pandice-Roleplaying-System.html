Pandice Roleplaying System (v. 0.8)

0. Foreword

This system is designed to be a free, light-to-the-touch, beginner-friendly roleplaying system. Thank you for using Pandice, I hope you enjoy it!

1. How to Play

The Big Picture

The Dungeon Master (DM) narrates a scene - usually by describing a situation, setting, or interaction that the players (party) find themselves in.

"As the butler sweeps you further into the mansion, the lights grow dim. Cobwebs hang overhead, and the smell of old wallpaper fills the air. Your footsteps squelch on the carpet - almost as if the mansion itself is unwilling to let go..."

Players can ask questions (for context) and commit to actions. They interact with the scene as their character would.

"Do I notice anything strange about this situation?"

"Make a Perception check, and we'll see what happens."

Roll to Find Out

Players roll dice to determine the success or failure of their character's actions.

"I rolled a 10, so that's a success."

After the roll, the DM continues the story, adding in the effects of player actions.

"You notice the butler's pocket has a paper hanging out, with the words 'They must arrive by nightfall' written on it."

Players work together to succeed. Roleplaying sessions are alive and fluid. Success brings experience, growth, and character advancement. With creativity and mindfulness, problems can be solved with both violence and non-violence.

2. Rolling Dice

Note: This game is played with two six-sided dice (2D6).

2a. Roll the Dice, Add Skill Values

Players roll 2D6 to get a base value, then add their relevant skill value.

Ex. To swing a sword, John rolls a 6, then adds his Strength value of 3 to get a 9.

2b. Success and Failure

Success and failure is grouped as follows:

10 | 11 | 12 : Success - "You do what you wanted to do, within reason"
07 | 08 | 09 : Partial Success - "You get at least a part of what you wanted, or it comes with a caveat"
04 | 05 | 06 : Failure - "You do not do what you wanted to do, sometimes with an additional penalty"
01 | 02 | 03 : Serious Failure - "The penalty is severe (but it will make your story more interesting)"

2c. Advantage and Disadvantage

The DM will sometimes assign a temporary status to players:

Advantage: Add +1 to your next roll.
Disadvantage: Subtract -1 from your next roll.

3. Character Creation

1. Select one Action Skill to specialize (add +1 to that skill).
2. Select one Social Skill to specialize in.
3. Select one Magic Skill to specialize in. (Optional)
4. Choose one Main Item , one Luxury Item, and add ten Gold to your inventory.
5. Give your character a name , appearance , and backstory. You're done!

(Note: No player can start a campaign good at everything! It's often a good idea to try to have different members of a party specialize in different things - it tends to encourage teamwork!)

4. Skills

Skills are abilities used in rolls and character actions. They start at a value of zero, and gain +1 for each specialization they receive. With a maximum value of +3, a skill can be specialized three times before reaching it's maximum value.

4a. Action Skills

(Note: Action Skills can be used both in and out of combat!)

Strength: Physicality, muscle power
Swing a sword, lift a crate

Fortitude: Grit, physical hardiness
Withstand poison, avoid being blown over by wind

Markspersonship: Accuracy and precision with aiming
Throwing a stone, loosing a bow

Dexterity: Deftness, coordination
Fence with a rapier, play the drums

Reflexes: Nimbleness, acrobatics
Flip over a fence, dodge a punch

Sneak: Stealth, thievery
Pick a pocket, eavesdrop unnoticed

Science: Research, invention
Crafting explosives, building a bridge

Worldliness: Worldly knowledge and experience
Local culture, ancient traditions

4b. Social Skills

Deception: Lying, misdirection
Faking your death, doing a magic trick

Empathy: Caring for others, understanding feelings
Listening to woes, mediating a conflict

Charisma: Being likable, winning people over
Convincing a skeptic, telling a joke

Intimidation: Threats, forcing your will
Taunting an enemy, blackmailing a politician

Perception: Observing, checking, attentiveness
Noticing a guard's tattoo, seeing a trap

4c. Magic Skills

White Magic: Healing, blessing, restoring
Healing plants, purifying water

Red Magic: Offensive, energy-based magic
Creating flames, shocking with lightning

Black Magic: Mysterious, vampiric/eldritch magic
Summoning tentacles, turning into a bat

5. Items and Gold

Items

Each character has a "main item" - often a signature weapon or key tool.
An ancient staff, a magic amulet, a favorite sword

Characters also start with a "luxury item" - usually an item of personal significance.
A letter from a loved one, a lucky hat, a favorite book

Players should expect to come across new weapons, items, and gear as they go. A player can carry a maximum of ten items at a time.

Gold

Gold coins are the standard currency in Pandice games, but that can be changed by the DM to suit a setting as needed. (Perhaps currency would be cans of gasoline, pages of sheet music, or crackers!) Players can carry as much gold as they wish.

6. Life, Death, and The Brink

Health and Healing

Each character starts with 10 Health Points (HP) which are removed when taking damage or being injured, and added when resting or healing. Healing can come in many forms - a good meal, health potions, or a good night's sleep, for example. Players should keep in mind that resting is dangerous in the field - a party may wake up to find their items stolen, or worse!

A standard health potion or healing spell restores 4 HP. More powerful items or spells could be created by a DM as they please!

The Brink

When a player's HP is 0, they are at The Brink - an unconscious and vulnerable state. A character at The Brink can be healed back to safe, conscious health, but it also adds a Strike (marked on their character sheet). A character can be revived twice from The Brink, but the third Strike is always fatal. Three strikes, you're out! (Alternatively, a strong attack to a character at The Brink will also prove fatal.)

Making New Characters

If a character dies, it's OK! Fellow party members can recover the character items (if possible), and the player can create a new character (leveled to the current lowest level in the party), rejoining the adventure as soon as possible.

7. Leveling Up and Progression

Level Up

When a player reaches 20 XP, they have leveled up! They add 1 HP to their current amount, and can also specialize another skill. (Remember, skills can be raised to a maximum of +3!)
