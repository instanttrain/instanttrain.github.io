Reviews

My rubric:
"   ": Probably will not watch/play/listen again. (Not indicative of quality - I'm just picky!)
"☆": Recommended. Entertaining and/or meaningful.
"★": Outstanding. A special artistic experience. (Note: this does not signify perfection - merely the highest score I can give it.)

Kirby Star Allies

Reviewed: 8/5/2025 | Score:

Solidly built, but didn't move me much emotionally. The Friend Heart system mostly remixes ideas from other Kirby games (Super Star's Helpers, Amazing Mirror's four-Kirbys, etc.). The end result is fun gameplay that series veterans will feel like they've seen it before.

Some minor game feel issues - Friend Abilities should be a button press instead of an analog stick tilt, Friend Hearts should move at a more satisfying speed. Lore is good.

The Mystic Knights of Tir Na Nog

Reviewed: 8/20/2025 | Score:

An unintentionally hilarious blend of Saban cheese and nondescript high fantasy. Just dreadful television, but I howled with laughter all the way through.

Porco Rosso

Reviewed: 8/5/2025 | Score: ★

As ever with Studio Ghibli: awe-inspiring in motion, equal parts dashing and crestfallen. Considerably nuanced, with both the beauty and ugliness of life on full display. Cigarettes and coastal wine. My favorite Ghibli film.

Ninajirachi - I Love My Computer

Reviewed: 9/3/2025 | Score: ☆

Really caused me to stop and reflect on what a unique existence it was to grow up in the Frutiger Aero 2000's (and how distanced that is to modern-day internet culture). An excellent love letter to what was.

I Am Big Bird

Reviewed: 9/12/2025 | Score:

A documentary of humility and bittersweetness (as stories that honestly talk about life are). A lovely exploration of the connection between artist and character.

The Epic of Gilgamesh (David Ferry translation)

Reviewed: 9/15/2025 | Score: ☆

Surprising just how complete it is. I don't get to read stories in verse often. A compelling meditation on change and death. I'm glad this is the oldest recorded story.

Survivor: Pearl Islands

Reviewed: 9/17/2025 | Score:

Solid television. A great cast, with a simpler, more straightforward version of Survivor. A great reminder of how important theming is for framing player mindsets.

Scooby Doo on Zombie Island

Reviewed: 9/24/2025 | Score:

Much different to watch as an adult than a child. Beautifully animated and charming, but the inclusion of Confederate soldiers and plantations does add a bit of messiness to the ethics of the whole thing.

The Three Stooges (2012)

Reviewed: 9/25/2025 | Score:

This seems like a concept doomed to fail, right? Excellent effort by the leading actors, but way too much plot - this would be much better off as a string of gag setpieces with an excuse plot. There's a few issues here - convoluted plot, supporting cast that doesn't bring the right rhythm, sound effects that sound a little crackly, but man, watching this one is like watching a plane fall out of the sky.
