Grief

A car crash. A lonely house. An aching soul. Grief is a first-person exploration game designed by a team of five for RPI's Experimental Game Design in two weeks. The player controls a blind individual, struggling with the five stages of grief after a sudden loss. The player can reach out and touch his environment, revealing the layout of his home and uncovering narrative information. I created art assets, designed levels, and was responsible for sound design for the game (including musical composition). The game boasts five levels, unique shaders, a full soundtrack, and level design incorporating non-Euclidian space. The download link can be located [here.]

In a Timely Fashion

Created for RPI's Game Development I 2015 with the Unreal Engine in three weeks, In a Timely Fashion is a first-person shooter puzzle game. With the theme of Time Travel, our team wanted to make a more comedic take on the mechanic. Our game tells the story of a cleaning company sent to houses with only seconds remaining, using robot workers armed with state-of-the-art time-traveling technology. The player moves around the stage, shooting "cleaning fluid" at stains, broken appliances, uneven furniture, and other household messes. As the timer runs out at the top of the screen (about 10 seconds), the player picks another point on the map to spawn, and the timer resets. However, the player must take care not to shoot themselves, or bump into a past self while cleaning. The stage can get pretty hectic with 3 or 4 past selves shooting blasts all around you! I made a lot of art for this game in 3 weeks, including animations, models, cutscenes, UI, and other necessities. This is one of the best games I've ever made, and I'm thrilled that it was polished to an enjoyable level. My asset demo reel of this game. The game can be downloaded here.
